Welcome to OnVision

A Solution to automate the manual surveillance systems in Sri Lanka using Machine Learning.

A prominent digital approach is the usage of CCTV to gather video and audio data about the environments with high security concerns through remote observation instead of physical observation. In a country like Sri Lanka security status constantly requires maintaining public order and provide community safety.

Our area of focus is to automatically detect Fires, Road Accidents, White Line Violation on Roads and Public Unrest using the CCTV surveillance.

Main Services provide by our system are automatic detection of the incidents and issuing detailed alerts real-time which will benefit the security officials in the country to take necessary and immediate actions.

Literature Survey

Fires, Traffic rule violations, Accidents, and Civil unrest are the most prominent areas in Sri Lanka where people tend to break rules and disasters happen due to misfocus. Identification of Fires, Traffic Rule violations mainly based on White lines, Traffic Accident detection and Civil unrest situations involving fights need to be implemented to match the objectives of the proposed system. Many expert systems have been invented to identify and react productively against the selected security concerns. Most of the internationally conducted research just focus on one security area unlikely the conducted research which focus on multiple different security scenarios. Research conducted to identify fires from CCTVs has focused on identifying the fire activations using the initial states of fire [1], the lengths and the spread area of fires [2] and color and the shape of fire crystals [3] using R-CNN algorithmic approaches. For Traffic rule violation most of the conducted research centered around Tracking individual vehicles [4], get detailed traffic information for individual vehicles [4], use automatic detection line calculation algorithm to detect detailed vehicle movements [4], apply traffic conflict evaluation techniques for the tracking VIPS [5] and usage of (RFID) technology which uses radio waves for identifying objects and speed [6]. The researchers have identified various systems to improve successful accident detection systems. Mainly western countries have researched about various scenarios in accident detection. Most of the conducted research and developed systems well defined around accident that take place in highways and traffic situations [7], usage of object tracking methods to track each vehicle [7], detecting the shape of vehicles, addressing the lighting issues that can affect to accident identification process [8] and detecting the angle of the vehicle and speed change before declaring it as an accident [9]. Highly utilized technologies for stated processes are R-CNN (Region-based Convolutional Neural Network), YOLO v3 algorithm, and DETR (Detection Transformer), Canny edge detection algorithms. Mostly the unrest detection is considered as a form of human action and form of violence in already conducted research. Most violent detection research has taken Classifying situations as violent or non-violent [10], focus on the crowds and non-crowd-based scenarios [11], Analysis of long video clips [12] using deep learning concepts like 3D CNN, 2D CNN, and LSTM (long short-term memory), convLSTM (convolutional long-short memory).

Research Gap

From 2010 onwards Sri Lanka Police declared open a CCTV unit considering the past historical events like terrorism which highly affected the country’s security. According to Sri Lanka police web resources, CCTV surveillance is used to establish the country’s security in many aspects. There is no record of using CCTV footage for Fires, Road Accidents, Traffic rule violations, and Civil Unrest detection in real-time or automatically considered in Sri Lanka. The current prevailing system of Sri Lanka completely dwells on manual supervision done by an administrative panel. The most common approach is using CCTV clips of unrest behavior for post-investigation purposes. Also, most of the internationally proposed methodologies has not deployed as a system and many of them depend on technologies like CNN s which are proven not very ideal for a real-time environment.
Summary,
• Lack of an automated system in Sri Lanka.
• Lack Real-time identification and alert generation in Sri Lanka.
• Absence of an effective notification generation.
• Utilization of technologies that supports the real-time environment.
• Covering several security aspects through one system.



Research Problem & Solution

Problems
Due to urbanization, busy lifestyle, loss of values and ethics among people the incidents related to Fires, Traffic rule violation, Accidents, and Civil unrest records high occurrence rate in the country. 5% increase of crime rates compared to past years and Sri Lanka is ranked 96th in the world for the number of accidents that occur, with a fatality rate of 2.86 percent. Identified Problems due to the present situation that affects day-to-day life of citizens are,
• How to accurately detect fires and its characteristics and the origin location?
• How to accurately detect accidents its location and weather information?
• How to accurately detect white line violation on roads, location of the incident?
• How to accurately detect an unrest behavior scenario and its location?

Solution
The research conducted to implement a CCTV Based Enhanced Public Security Management System for Sri Lanka using machine learning and image processing. Implementation followed the addressing of areas of concern regarding to security in Sri Lanka highlighted in the identified research problems. As the solution an approach using YOLO V5 algorithms for Detection of Fires, Road Accidents, Civil Unrest situation and White line violation on roads is developed. Problem of issuing alerts for respective officials also exist during a detection of selected security factors. Administrators of the existing system has heavy workload due to manual supervision of CCTV feeds and lack of proper notification generation. As the further approach to the solution an efficient alert system is also integrated to the developed solution using the SMS gateway, Textit API including detailed alert information of severity classification, location and weather details obtained using OpenWeather and Google APIs.



Research Objectives




Automatic Detection of Fires
During an incident of fire disaster, the automatic system needs to respond in real time by identifying the situation. The response is to be send an alert to fire brigade, hospitals, and police CCTV division. The system's primary target is to accurately recognize and differentiate real-time fire incidents in Sri Lanka using additional information based on the environmental condition and wind direction and calculate the risk of fire spreading it as a percentage.

Automatic Detection of Accidents in Roads
One of the objectives of implementing the proposed system is automate the current manual process of accident identification in Sri Lanka. The automated process can respond in real-time when accident happen. The response is to be send an alert to hospitals and police CCTV division, by considering the several facts like weather condition, road condition and correctly identifying the location. The results are presented a percentage of detection and severity levels.

Automatic Detection of White Line Violation on Roads
One of the main purposes of the system is to identify White line violation around intersection points on the road using an automated rule violation system. The response is to be send an alert to Traffic police division and police CCTV division to take necessary action regarding the violators on time using their location of violation.  The solution has the mechanism of monitoring the intersections and violations will be tracked by an algorithm and the number plate of the vehicle will be captured which helps the traffic police department to issue fines for the violators.

Automatic Detection of Public Unrest Behavior
The final objective of the system is to identify the unrest situations related to the street fighting on the roads or public places. Identification proceeds with detecting and localizing regions related to the unrest behavior from the incoming video frames from the CCTV stream and issuing a confident score for the detection. Presence of the human figures and fighting is analyzed and alerts get issued in to the Criminal Investigations department and CCTV division of the Sri Lanka police using the severity levels and the detected location.



Methodology

The purposed CCTV based surveillance system is contain four main components according to the detections.
1. Fire detection and analysis
2. Traffic rule violation detection
3. Accident detection and analysis
4. Human …... detection
The system diagram of Fig 2 has shown the high-level approach that the system has developed with the several technologies and techniques. The CCTV camara feed take into the system and convert to set of frames which are the input for Flask server system. The frames have redirected to the four components and the YOLO V5 object detection model will identify the main objects relevant to the incidents from subsequent frames and extract those frames. As shown in the diagram the extracted frames have further analyzed to get details of the incident. The location of the incident is one of the details that get from IP address of the CCTV camera. The current weather condition in incident location takes from Open Weather API. Alerts will generate including the identified details and send to the registered users using Text It SMS API. The main users of the system which is Sri Lanka Police CCTV Division use the administration application and the incident notification and details will preview in the application. The other users like hospitals and fire bridged can register to the system from admin application. For those users could be able to get SMS notification in the real-time if incident detected. After incident detection the subsequent frames that include the incident will save in the Mongo database. Also, the record details will store as a log in separate cluster for retrieving purposes.
